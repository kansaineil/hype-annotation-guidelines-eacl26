### **1. Value-judgement**  
Usually a **value judgement** - adds a **prestige-signalling layer** (importance, foresight, alignment with high-level goals).

**Not a value judgement when relating to technical description of how cognition works (e.g. *strategic processes*)

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

*The overarching **strategic** plan of this SPORE is to develop methods for identifying persons at greatest risk for developing lung cancer and then preventing this progression .* 

- *Anxious individuals direct their attention toward threat during early , automatic stages of processing , whereas during later , more **strategic** stages of processing , they tend to direct their attention away from threat .*


</details>

### **3a. Gratuitous**  
Almost always **gratuitous** as it predominantly occurs in attributive constructions. Common with abstract organizational nouns (e.g. *plan, planning, goal, vision, objective, priorities, initiative, approach, direction, decision, focus, area, investment, development, management, leadership, framework, timing*)


<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *Consistent with this **strategic** goal , the thematic aims of the DT Program are to : 1 ) discover new molecular targets for cancer therapy ; 2 ) develop new agents and biomarkers ; and 3 ) translate new therapies and biomarkers into clinical trials .*

- *A number of highly **strategic** applications of this tool can be envisioned for basic research , drug development , and patient management .*  

- *The overarching **strategic** plan of this SPORE is to develop methods for identifying persons at greatest risk for developing lung cancer and then preventing this progression .*  

</details>

### **3b. Amplified**  
Rarely intensified by adverbs.

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *A number of highly **strategic** applications of this tool can be envisioned for basic research , drug development , and patient management .*  

</details>

---

### **3c. Broader context**  
Sometimes **stacked**, with other hype adjectives (e.g. *important, key*)
Often occurs with other hype adjectives in same sentence (e.g. key, major, important, novel, critical, essential, strategic, effective, significant, relevant, extensive, comprehensive)

<details markdown="1">
<summary><em>Examples</em></summary>

- *The Steering Committee will make key **strategic** decisions and engage in program planning and review on a day - to - day basis .*  

- *The scientific **strategic** purpose of the continuation of this COBRE is to support research in immunology and inflammation that impacts human health and disease .*

- *The **strategic** goal of this revised proposal is to put this critical technology in the hands of a two highly productive groups of neuroscientists at Northwestern University ( NU ) that receive nearly $ 8M / year ( direct costs ) in funding from NINDS .*

</details>
